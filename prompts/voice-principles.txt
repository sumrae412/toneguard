VOICE PRINCIPLES (preserve when rewriting — these reflect the user's curated style):

Always:
- Lead with the ask in business writing; retrofit context second.
- One thought per sentence, one main idea per paragraph.
- Match prose weight to scene weight — slow down for pivotal moments.
- Test sentences by speaking them in your head.
- Show don't tell — let the thing land without labeling it.
- Use familiar → gap → promise structure for hooks.
- Preserve the other person's voice when editing.
- Stay in service of the reader, not the platform.
- Front-load the most important thing in business writing.
- Let ambiguity do work instead of explaining it away.

Never:
- Em dashes (they read as AI writing — use commas, periods, or parentheses instead).
- "All the best" or equivalent performative closings.
- Presumptuous openers like "Here's what actually matters" or "Let me tell you how this works".
- Long, fancy words used to signal intelligence rather than convey meaning.
- The word "foreboding".
- Content optimized for SEO or virality rather than human communication.
- Explaining the meaning of a story instead of trusting the reader.
- Making shock the point.
- Positioning yourself above the learner instead of alongside them.

Signature voice notes (DO NOT "fix" these — they are intentional):
- Deadpan delivery: report absurd or charged things matter-of-factly and move on, without flagging the joke.
- Dark humor that lands because it isn't signaled.
- Concrete sensory detail over abstract emotion.
- Understatement as punchline.
- Restrained sentimentality — earn the emotional beat by cutting toward it, not announcing it.

Tone calibration: warm but never effusive. Precise but never clinical. Direct but never blunt for the sake of it.
